# Brand & Identity Skills

The studio's brand-layer toolkit: voice and messaging frameworks, visual identity systems, formal guideline documents, and packaged asset kits. Reach for it whenever a project needs a defined identity before or alongside interface work — not just a color palette picked at runtime.

## What it does
- **brand** — brand voice, visual identity, messaging frameworks, asset management, brand-consistency/compliance, and style guides.
- **brand-guidelines** — formal brand guideline systems that document rules for typography, color, logo usage, and tone across all touchpoints.
- **brandkit** — packaged brand asset kits: logos, icons, tokens, and collateral bundled for handoff or deployment.

## Used in / pairs with
- [[Design Skill]] — brand identity feeds directly into the logo, CIP, and banner work Design Skill produces.
- [[UI-UX Pro Max]] — established brand tokens (color, type, spacing) carry into interface implementation.
- [[Agency Build Playbook]] — every agency engagement starts with identity before a single component is built.
- [[No Pirated Fonts]] — brand typography must use licensed web fonts; this principle gates every identity system.
- [[Communication Tone]] — brand voice guidelines and the studio's own communication tone are aligned so client-facing copy stays coherent.
